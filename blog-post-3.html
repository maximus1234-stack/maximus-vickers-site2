PERSONAL GROWTH

My Entrepreneurship Journey at the University of Miami

My journey began at Miami Dade College with a 4.0 and a lot of ambition. Moving from MDC to the University of Miami was more than an academic transfer; it was a shift in mindset from mastering coursework to building something that could actually change the way people experience music.

At UM, I balanced classes with late nights working on FourMeasure. The campus community and classroom discussions helped me think more strategically, while the challenges of running a startup forced me to move faster and make decisions under pressure. That combination was invaluable.

Founding a tech company as a student taught me how to lead with both curiosity and discipline. Every pitch, every user interview, and every product iteration helped me grow. The lessons I learned at MDC and UM were not just academic—they were the foundation for real-world execution.

Personal growth came from the moments I had to choose between comfort and progress. Whether it was stepping into a new networking room, asking for feedback, or pivoting product direction, each decision pushed me beyond what felt familiar. That growth is what defined my college experience more than any grade.

Looking back, I see a clear arc from MDC to UM to FourMeasure. Each stage brought new perspective, new responsibility, and new momentum. The journey continues, but the lessons from those early days remain the ones I return to when I need direction.
